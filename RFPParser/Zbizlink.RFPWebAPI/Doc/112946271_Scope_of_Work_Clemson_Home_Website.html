I. SCOPE OF SOLICITATION

 

Introduction 

Clemson is a public four-year university in Clemson, South Carolina offering undergraduate and graduate degrees delivered in both the traditional classroom setting and through online courses.  Ranked as the 24th best national public university by U.S.News & World Report, Clemson is a science- and engineering-oriented college dedicated to teaching, research and service. 

Clemson Home is a division of Clemson University Student Affairs. We are the Housing & Dining arm of the University including: housing, dining, residential living, residential learning, maintenance and custodial. 

 

The design, navigation and programming of our current site is outdated.  Clemson Home seeks to overhaul the design, structure, and functionality of its website to be responsive on multiple devices and screens, including desktop, tablet and smartphone.  The vendor will develop a new website with the primary goal of communicating the benefits of living on campus.  In addition to graphic design, navigation, and CMS integration, the vendor will provide guidance and assistance throughout the entire process, including concept development, gathering stakeholder input, construction, migration of content, testing, training for key website content editors, and implementation of maintenance methods. 

 

The vision is to have an interactive, attractive, easily navigable website that will be intuitive for new students and their families to use while encouraging engagement. Secondarily, current students will be able to easily locate the information and services they require, and employees can navigate the services and resources they need. 

 

The new site will reinforce Clemson Home’s visual branding and identity guidelines. Additionally, the new site will provide administrators with the ability to monitor and track analytics on viewers’ behaviors. 

 

All website solutions are welcomed for any required functionality or features. The existing Clemson Home website is built on WordPress.

 

 

Clemson University Housing & Dining (Clemson Home) is seeking to redesign its website, https://housing.clemson.edu/.

 

You have been selected as a vendor to prepare a proposal that includes timeline, cost, and deliverables. The following RFP includes a background of our organization and describes the purpose of the redesign, its desired functionality, and specific requests relating to the proposal. We understand that details may be subject to change upon vendor recommendation and/or research of more optimal solutions. In your proposal, please feel free to suggest alternatives that meet or exceed the descriptions.

 

Award

The award will be made to one Offeror. Award will be made to the highest ranked, responsive and responsible Offeror whose offer is determined to be the most advantageous to the University.  

 

Contract Period

The purpose of this RFP is to establish a contract with a qualified vendor to deliver a completed product within 120 days of finalizing the agreement.  The contract should specify creative and technical services that will be provided in the development as well as technical support for website maintenance, training, and troubleshooting after the initial launch. Clemson Home will own the right to use all technologies and creative designs and/or features used in this project. 

 

Any resulting contract will begin on the date specified in the notice of award.  The effective date of this contract is the first day of the Maximum Contract Period as specified in the final statement of award. For bids where award statement is not required, the effective date of the contract will be the issue date on the Purchase Order.

III. SCOPE OF WORK / SPECIFICATIONS

 

The scope of work requires a complete overhaul of the existing website. The need is a full and complete redesign from discovery to launch. The project proposal should include everything that is necessary for the delivery of a website that is visually attractive and functions well. These are a few things we expect to see addressed in the vendor response: process for finalizing the visual design, specific technology/development (front-end and back-end), information architecture, user experience, content strategy, content organization, content development (such as copy, photography, and/or video production), content migration/uploading, and more.  The Contractor shall perform an initial audit of the current website prior to initiating any development. When submitting the line item budget detail, please note timeline, tasks and deliverables that Clemson Home could provide that would create savings. 

 

Other requirements within the scope include:

 

Functionality

The final product will include the following features:

•      Content Management System (CMS)

•      Hosted in the Cloud

•      Contact Form(s) (custom code or third-party application)

•      Multimedia Integration (graphics, photography, video, etc.)

•      Events Calendar

•      Blog

•      Directory

•      Short URLs (this is not to include sub-domain names)

•      Interactive Campus Map

•      RSS feed for breaking news and alerts

•      News/Press Releases

It is encouraged that vendors review the existing website to see what other functionality or features are needed or may be useful for our website visitors. Please plan for any other proposed web solutions and make note of it as a separate line item in the summary pricing or project cost.

Design and Navigation 

The Clemson Home website needs a modern, visually-pleasing design appropriate for an institution of higher education that includes, but not limited to:

•      Ease of use and intuitive navigability

•      Consistent and logical organization of pages and use of nomenclature across all pages

•      Link to room selection software (StarRez)

•      Link to Dining services

•      Integration of Clemson Home’s primary social media platforms, including Facebook, Twitter, YouTube and Instagram into its web presence

•      Responsive design for functionality on mobile devices

•      Creative integration of Clemson Home’s visual branding and identity guidelines

•      Clemson Home will consider suggestions on areas where content should be revised and/or replaced (eg. infographics). In those cases, the Contractor will be responsible for the design, development and deployment of such graphics and/or elements.

Specific Guidelines

•      Provide recommendations on consolidating or deleting pages on existing website

•      Allow for the embedding of video, audio, and other interactive media on any given page

•      Departmental pages will showcase staff (as appropriate)

•      Interactive calendar of events

•      Map all current top-level content from old system URLs to new system URLs

•      Content management system should allow site administrators to restrict author formatting rights

•      Design an interactive map of Clemson’s campus indicating housing and dining locations, specifically

•      Provide three (3) conceptual designs for the website home page, each indicative of the design style proposed for the remaining pages on the site

Accessibility

The site should be designed to be accessible to persons with disabilities and fully compliant with the technical standards and functional performance criteria of Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act.  

 

Compatibility

•      The Contractor must utilize HTML5 and CSS techniques, so the website displays effectively and uniformly on all browsers, including but not limited to Internet Explorer (with graceful degradation for older versions), Chrome, Firefox, Opera, and Safari.

•      The Contractor must provide a responsive site that scales appropriately to display effectively on desktops, notebooks, tablets, and smartphones.

•      The website (including plug-ins) must be built with industry-standard languages/constructs and allow for maintenance using readily available tools. Details of these tools, languages and constructs must be provided in the proposal.         

Conversion

Proposals should include a detailed plan and timeline for launching the new site. Clemson Home has an extensive library of quality photos and videos available to support page development. 

 

Maintenance 

The new website should be structured in such a way that Clemson Home staff, with limited web design experience, can maintain, update, and modify text media and page design across all pages, and fix basic problems that arise in the routine checking of links, images, and code.  The proposer should outline how updates and maintenance to the launched site will be handled for the first 180 days of being live. The proposal should also include costs and availability for proposer to provide general maintenance as needed beyond the initial 180 days. 

 

Search Engine Optimization 

Ensure Clemson Home website exposure by adhering to the latest search engine optimization best practices and submitting page and sitemap information to all popular search engines (Google, Bing, Yahoo, Safari, etc.) while allowing integration with social media platforms. 

 

Future Customization 

New website should allow for the flexibility to significantly redesign pages in the future. 

 

Customer Service and Training 

Introductory training of key content editors is required. The availability of no-charge phone and email access for Clemson Home staff to contact Customer Service is expected.

 

Launch

The Contractor must receive final approval from Clemson Home on all aspects of the website prior to the Go Live Date (this is the date that the website goes from testing into production and is available to external users through the internet).  

 

Communication

The Contractor shall:

a)      conduct calls based on an agreed-upon schedule regarding progress creating and implementing projects,

b)      provide monthly progress reports to include budget reports, project effectiveness, and web/digital analytics,

c)      provide monthly planning sessions, as requested and based on need, and;

d)      provide oral or written presentations regarding strategic and creative plans, as requested by either the Contractor or by Clemson Home.
